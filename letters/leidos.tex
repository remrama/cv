Please consider my enthusiastic application for the Sleep Research Scientist position at Leidos.

I am currently a postdoctoral fellow at the Center for Advanced Research in Sleep Medicine, one of Canada's top sleep clinics and a highly collaborative research center. Here, I am continuing my work on the cognitive neuroscience of sleep that I conducted during my PhD at the University of Texas and subsequent postdoc working with Dr. Ken Paller at Northwestern University.

Over the course of my career I have built a reputation amongst my peers as a passionate researcher who explores new directions in sleep science with enthusiasm. I believe my strong publication record and varied methodological approaches make me a strong candidate for the Sleep Research Scientist position at Leidos. In this role I could have a strong impact on the important work being done in the US Navy's Warfighter Performance Department to improve the health and performance of military personnel.

I was introduced to such work when I collaborated with Leidos and the Warfighter Performance Department during my first postdoc. Working closely with the Warfighter Performance Department, I ran statistical models exploring the complex relationships between data collected from questionnaires and personal status monitors from US Navy personnel. This work led to novel observations about fatigue and nightmares in military operations~(Mallett~et~al.,~2023,~\textit{SLEEP}) and I would be excited to continue related directions as a Sleep Research Scientist at Leidos.
